# Nameless Cemetery Association: Brand Guide

**Domain**: namelesscemetery.org
**Tagline**: Est. 1882. Preserved by community.

---

## Brand Essence

Rooted. Dignified. Warm. Not corporate, not morbid, not churchy. This is a rural Texas Hill Country cemetery maintained by neighbors and descendants. The brand should feel like the iron gate itself: handmade, enduring, quietly beautiful.

## Typography

### Headings: Playfair Display (Google Fonts)
- Weight: 700 (bold), 400 (regular for subtitles)
- Feel: Carved stone, iron lettering, historical gravitas
- Fallback: Georgia, "Times New Roman", serif

### Body: Source Sans 3 (Google Fonts)
- Weight: 400 (regular), 600 (semibold for emphasis)
- Feel: Clean, readable, modern but not cold
- Fallback: "Segoe UI", Roboto, sans-serif

### Monospace (code, metadata): JetBrains Mono
- Only if needed for burial record IDs or technical elements
- Fallback: "Courier New", monospace

### Scale
- h1: 2.5rem / 700
- h2: 1.875rem / 700
- h3: 1.375rem / 700
- body: 1.0625rem / 400 (17px base for readability)
- small: 0.875rem
- caption: 0.8125rem

## Logo

The gate arch silhouette is the primary mark. The iron arch reading "NAMELESS CEMETERY" is the most recognizable visual element of the site. The logo is a simplified SVG silhouette of this arch.

### Variants
1. **Full mark**: Arch silhouette + "Nameless Cemetery Association" text below
2. **Icon mark**: Arch silhouette only (for favicon, social, small spaces)
3. **Text mark**: "NAMELESS CEMETERY" in Playfair Display, small caps (for headers where the icon is already present)

### Logo Colors
- Primary: Iron (#2C2C2C) on Limestone (#F5F0E8)
- Reversed: Limestone (#F5F0E8) on Dark (#1A1A1A)
- Single color: works in pure black or pure white

---

## Photography

- Travis's gate photo is the hero image
- Cemetery photos should be warm-toned, not bleak
- Golden hour or overcast preferred over harsh midday
- Focus on: headstones, live oaks, the gate, the schoolhouse, community events, people
- Avoid: dark/gothic treatment, heavy filters, desaturated looks

---

## Tone of Voice

- Plain language. Carolyn and her generation should understand everything.
- Warm but not saccharine. This is about preservation, not grief.
- Historical facts stated simply. Let the stories speak.
- Community-first: "we" not "the organization"
- No jargon, no nonprofit-speak, no marketing language
- If it wouldn't sound right said aloud at the schoolhouse, rewrite it.

---

## Print Considerations

- Logo must work in B&W (flyers, newspaper, photocopies)
- Body text minimum 12pt for print (audience skews older)
- High contrast: Iron on Limestone or White
- QR codes link to namelesscemetery.org (not Facebook, not WordPress)
